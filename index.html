Close to Lost

Ainu is a language that has no known relatives or families. For this reason, it borrows japanese scripts. Dropping as low as 2 native speakers, attempts to revitilize the language are being made, but it is still considered critically endangered. Typefaces such as Noto Sans are important for preserving languages like Ainu, as they continue to support and include these glyphs in their font families.

"Dead" languages

The Norse language is considered a dead language, but it is still used in some parts of the world. The Elder Futhark is a runic alphabet that was used by the Norse people. The alphabet is still used today by some people who practice Norse paganism. The Noto typface works to support languages such as these, as they are a part of our history, but also part of modern cultural practices.
